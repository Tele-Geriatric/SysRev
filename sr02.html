Structured Document Summary

SLEEP AND MCI RESEARCH TOOLKIT - STRUCTURED DOCUMENT

BACKGROUND:
Emerging evidence suggests bidirectional relationships between sleep disturbances and mild cognitive impairment (MCI). Sleep architecture changes (reduced slow-wave and REM sleep) often precede MCI diagnosis, while sleep disorders (insomnia, sleep apnea) may accelerate cognitive decline. Potential mechanisms include amyloid clearance during deep sleep, tau pathology propagation, and memory consolidation impairment. Understanding these relationships offers opportunities for early intervention and cognitive preservation.

RESEARCH OPPORTUNITIES:
1. Longitudinal sleep architecture changes and MCI progression
2. Optimal sleep duration thresholds in MCI
3. Circadian interventions for cognitive outcomes
4. Sleep-disordered breathing and AD biomarkers
5. Comparative effectiveness of sleep medications
6. Sleep fragmentation and domain-specific decline
7. Cultural variations in sleep perception
8. Sleep spindles and memory consolidation
9. APOE-sleep intervention interactions
10. Cost-effectiveness of sleep screening
